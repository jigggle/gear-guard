# Equipment Inspection SOP

## Purpose

This document defines a basic inspection workflow for football equipment items flagged by GearGuard.

GearGuard is a decision-support tool. It does not replace manual inspection by trained equipment staff.

---

## Risk Tiers

### Critical

Recommended action:

- Remove item from active rotation immediately.
- Perform full physical inspection.
- Check prior repair history.
- Compare against similar items from the same vendor or batch.
- Do not return item to use until cleared.

### High

Recommended action:

- Prioritize item for inspection before next usage cycle.
- Review condition, repair history, fit complaints, and usage exposure.
- Monitor similar items from the same equipment type or vendor.

### Medium

Recommended action:

- Monitor during routine inspection.
- Review if multiple medium-risk items appear from the same vendor, batch, or season.

### Low

Recommended action:

- No immediate action required.
- Continue routine inspection schedule.

---

## Inspection Checklist

For flagged equipment, review:

- Visible wear
- Surface damage
- Structural integrity
- Fit complaints
- Repair history
- Prior inspection score
- Usage volume
- Game exposure
- Practice exposure
- Rain exposure
- Storage conditions
- Vendor or batch-level issues

---

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate the issue if:

- The item is critical risk.
- Multiple items from the same batch are high risk.
- Inspection scores are drifting downward.
- Repair frequency increases suddenly.
- Anomaly scores increase across a vendor or equipment type.
